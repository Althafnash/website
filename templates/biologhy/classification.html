Classification of animals

Grouping of animals into different levels based on their common characteristic is known as classification

Significance of classification of organisms

  • Easy to study about organisms
  • Ability to identify specific distinguish characteristic of an organisms
  • Ability to get a new idea about the whole biosphere
  • Ability to reveal different relationship with different organisms
  • Identification of organisms with economical use for human

Aristotle introduced the first scientific classification of organisms

Carolus Linnaenus in the 18th century introduced a successful classification method

There are two types of classification

  • Natural classification
  • Artificial classification

In artificial classification features such as presence or absence of locomotive appendix or habits etc are considered

It does not depend on evolutionary relations

A natural classification method depicts the evolutionary relationship among organisms

In natural classification morphological physiological and molecular biological features are considered

Natural classification

  • Explain natural relationship among organisms of some species
  • Explain the evolutionary relationship between organisms

Nomenclature is used to name animals scientifically

A successful Nomenclature was introduced by a swedish natural scientists called carolus Linnaeus in 1753

As it contained two epitelial it is known as binomial Nomenclature

The methodology of naming an organisms is regulated by The International commission of Botanical Nomenclature (ICBN) and the International commission of zoology Nomenclature(ICZN)

The standard of binomial Nomenclature

  • The scientific name of a species is composed of two epithelia
  • The first epithelial is genre name and the second ethel is the specific epitelial
  • The species name is given in Latin or Greek
  • The first letter of generic name is capital
