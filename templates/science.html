parts of a tanks

the dam of the tanks

  • the dam of the tank is constructed intercepting a river at a point which two hills were located close to each other on both banks of the river
  • the rivers in sri lanka goes dry in one season so using this opportunity they dug the soil and use it to make a dam
  • when a river is intercepted by constructing the dam across it
  • the upper section of the river is automatically turned into a tank when t is filled with water

  • iranamadu wewa

the sluice

  • the sulice is the main outlet point in which the water from the tank is left out to the network of canals
  • there are two or more sulice in a tank
  • there are two types of sulice
  • dry sluice
  • wet sulice
  • the dry sulice is used when the water level is high
  • the wet sulice is constructed at the lower level of the dam of the tank
  • it is helping in the releasing of water in the lower level

the bisokotuwa

  • the pressure generated by the volume and the velocity of water from a large and deep tank is variably exerts an extremely damaging impact on the dam of the tank
  • the bisokotuwa is in the built in the shape of a square well at a point at which the dam of the tank and water level meet to regular or totally stop the impact of the water pressure

ralapanawa

  • it is a relevant or pitching of flat stones slabs on the inner face of the dam of the tank
  • it reinforces the dam and act as a wave breaker and resists the action the wave play on the dam
  • the point at which the surplus water of the tank is let out
  • this is usually constructed with stone slabs and in the form of a gap at a lower point of the dam
  • it enables the excess water of the tank to pass over it without causing damage to the dam
